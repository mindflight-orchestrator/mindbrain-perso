## Chapter I — General provisions

### Article 1 — Subject matter

This Regulation lays down harmonised rules for the placing on the market, the putting into service, and the use of artificial intelligence systems in the Union.

It also prohibits certain artificial intelligence practices that are considered to present an unacceptable risk.

### Article 2 — Scope

This Regulation applies to providers placing on the market or putting into service artificial intelligence systems in the Union, irrespective of whether those providers are established within the Union or in a third country.

It also applies to deployers of artificial intelligence systems that have their place of establishment or are located within the Union.

## Chapter II — Prohibited artificial intelligence practices

The following artificial intelligence practices shall be prohibited:

(a) the placing on the market, the putting into service or the use of an AI system that deploys subliminal techniques beyond a person’s consciousness.

(b) the placing on the market, the putting into service or the use of an AI system that exploits vulnerabilities of a specific group of persons due to their age or disability.
